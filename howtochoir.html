How to Audition for BYU Choirs

A complete guide to help you prepare and audition

Audition Overview & Steps

  • Visit the BYU Choirs Auditions & FAQ page and fill out the audition survey when it opens.
  • Once you complete the survey, sign up for an audition time (either in-person or online).
  • Prepare the required hymn for your voice part (see “Hymn & Sheet Music Requirements” below).
  • On audition day (or for recorded audition) you'll do:
    • An a cappella performance of the selected hymn (one verse).
    • Vocal range check.
    • Sight-reading / tonal memory / ear training exercises.
  • If selected, you'll receive a permission-to-add code and can register for the choir class through BYU’s system.

About the Choirs

BYU Singers is a 40-voice mixed chamber choir renowned for its musical versatility and refined ensemble sound. Founded in 1984, the Singers perform works spanning classical, sacred, contemporary, and world music traditions, consistently earning acclaim for their artistry and precision.
BYU Concert Choir is a select mixed-voice ensemble of nearly 100 students from diverse majors. Under the leadership of Dr. Brent Wells, the choir performs choral-orchestral masterworks, traditional repertoire, multicultural selections, and newly commissioned works, showcasing both power and nuance.
BYU Men's Chorus, directed by Dr. Brent Wells, is the largest collegiate men’s choir in the United States with approximately 180 members. With a history dating back to the early 1900s, the ensemble performs sacred hymns, spirituals, and secular works, often collaborating with orchestras and other choirs.
BYU Women's Chorus, directed by Dr. Sonja Poulter, is a vibrant ensemble of over 160 women. The choir emphasizes expressive singing, technical excellence, and cohesive ensemble sound, presenting diverse repertoire across styles and eras.
